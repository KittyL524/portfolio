UNDERTAKER


This school group project involved 4 people making a Turn-Based Combat 3D game.

MY ROLE

In this project, my main role was to create visual assets, including character designs, scenes, UI elements, CG and 3D models. I also collaborated with my group members to develop a compelling storyline and gameplay mechanics.

CONCEPT

"Undertaker" is a third-person, turn-based strategy (TBS) game, introducing a unique hybrid combat system, blending real-time action with traditional turn-based mechanics to enhance game pacing. This approach provides a more dynamic and engaging experience compared to conventional turn-based games. Players engage in real-time dodging to accumulate energy and plan strategic attacks, creating a fast-paced and exciting gameplay experience.

The core objective of the game is to purify the soul by overcoming the emotional burdens accumulated throughout a lifetime. Players explore a fantasy world, uncover hidden memories, and face enemies through hybrid combat while solving puzzles to reveal the protagonist’s backstory.

PROCESS

Challenge

The game was originally designed with fully 3D characters and environments. However, during prototyping, we realized that the production scope exceeded our time constraints. To ensure project completion while maintaining visual appeal, we blended 3D as base with 2D paper-cut art style inspired by Don't Starve.

scenes screenshot

Visual Asset

I created the initial character drafts, CG illustrations, and UI designs using Procreate. I modeled the characters in Blender and integrated all visual assets into the game scenes using Unity. Additionally, I edited the game’s opening animation (excluding sound design) in After Effects.

The original design of the boss character “Clown” featured an exaggeratedly long hat that dragged on the ground, with the character walking hunched over using the two sharp corners of the hat and its legs. During prototyping, the hat was shortened to reduce the complexity and workload of costume physics and animation.

enemy design

CG, tutorial and UI

Since we incorporated extensive background narrative design into our narrative but lacked the time and assets to fully explain it within the gameplay, we created a short paper-cut style animation as an introduction, helping players to quickly immerse themselves in the game’s atmosphere. I was responsible for creating the animation, with the exception of sound design.

FINAL THOUGHTS

Despite the challenges and occasional stress, I truly enjoyed working on this project. It was a valuable learning experience that deepened my understanding of game design and development workflows. I was fortunate to work with a strong and collaborative team, where we developed effective teamwork, time management, and problem-solving skills. Through the process, we also realized the importance of finishing a basic idea before adding other big concept to it.
