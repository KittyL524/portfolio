MY ROLE

In this project, my main role was to create visual assets, including the map, UI elements, and game cards. I also collaborated with my team to design the economic system and was responsible for organizing playtesting sessions and gathering feedback to refine the gameplay.

CONCEPT

"Interastral Merchant" is a four-player game allows each player's ship a certain amount of storage for loading ore samples or raw stone from different stations. Players can sell the raw stone for money to carry out various actions and activities, such as mining or exchanging. The game includes star routes consisting of a certain number of planets. Players move between planets by piloting their ships with the distance traveled determined by the number of dice thrown. During their movement, players will encounter various events, some with positive outcomes and others negative. Players must decide on their actions, choose routes, and strategize based on the resources they have.

We designed this board game for ages 8 and up, catering to all genders. Our game appeals to BrainHex’s Achiever, who enjoys achieving in-game objectives and goals by optimizing strategies and resources. It also attracts BrainHex’s Mastermind, who delights in devising strategies to overcome the challenge of collecting samples before any other player with efficient decisions. Lastly, it engages BrainHex’s Socializer, who enjoys interacting with others in games to probe their opponents' strategies and plan accordingly.

It promotes strategic gameplay through its engaging mechanics, built on the principles of accumulating, converting, crafting, and trading. These elements are intertwined with core and secondary game mechanics to create a dynamic and immersive experience.

PROCESS

Design Choices

Interastral Merchant is designed with a resource-based economic system centered around four key activities: accumulating, converting, crafting, and trading. Players collect ores and samples through exploration and mining, convert resources into currency, and strategically invest in items, upgrades, or additional mining opportunities. This system encourages planning, risk assessment, and adaptive gameplay.

To balance chance and strategy, we used randomized movement by dice, event outcomes, and item draws—while allowing strategic mitigation through upgrades and card usage. Multiple mining locations and flexible routing ensure no dominant path. We implemented positive and negative feedback loops to reward smart investment such as upgrading storage while avoiding runaway leads by imposing inventory trade-offs and resource limitations, promoting both long-term planning and moment-to-moment decision-making.

Visual Asset

I created the initial map layout in Procreate and, after team discussions, optimized the number and arrangement of map grids to enhance the strategic depth of route selection. Additionally, I designed various game cards—including resources, items, and currency—based on the interstellar merchant theme and narrative of the board game.

FINAL THOUGHTS

Working on Interastral Merchant was both challenging and rewarding, especially since we only had about a month to complete it. Throughout the process, I got to dive into board game design, from crafting mechanics and building an economic system to creating visual assets. Collaborating with my team taught me how important time management, clear communication, and problem-solving are when you're working under pressure. Even with limited time, we managed to create a game that felt strategic, balanced, and visually consistent. It was a tough but incredibly valuable experience that really grew my understanding of game design.
